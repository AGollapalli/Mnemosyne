The mathematician learns to leave his head. 

The physician is tortured and rebels for a moral useless cause, but inspires a glimmer of morality for others. Do not comment whether this moralism is good or bad. It just is. I don’t know why we do it.

The sexpot is revealed to be a human being as well. Not a caricature. A petty jaded creature, but a sympathetic one. 

The career woman is ruined by her own thought processes. She had opportunities to embrace a secondary role and she left it. She rationalizes, but is deeply unhappy. She still has moments of happiness, but they seem in some way or another related to her desire to leave the reigns. She dislikes the burden.
